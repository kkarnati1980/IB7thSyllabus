# IISH MYP Grade 7 — Master Knowledge Base Index (2026–2027)

> **What this is.** A concept-by-concept, unit-by-unit knowledge base built from the IISH MYP Grade 7 Syllabus Focus (2026–2027). It is designed to power an IB MYP teaching assistant that teaches for understanding, inquiry, and transfer — not memorization. Every subject file is structured **Subject overview → Unit → Sub-topic (chapter) → Concept entries**, and every concept carries a 5-level explanation scaffold, real-world examples, analogies, misconceptions, IB concept tags, and command-term questions.
>
> **Start here, then open the framework file, then the subject you need.**

---

## How to use this knowledge base (for the teaching assistant)

1. **Discover the goal.** Identify the subject and unit the student is asking about, and open the matching subject file below.
2. **Ground the lesson.** Read the framework file (`00-IB-framework-reference.md`) for the definitions of Key Concepts, Global Contexts, ATL skills, Learner Profile, command terms, and assessment criteria.
3. **Locate the concept.** Each subject file is ordered Unit → Chapter → Concept. Jump to the concept and use its scaffold (definition → age-10 → classroom → MYP → cross-subject → advanced), example, analogy, misconception, and questions.
4. **Teach by inquiry.** Open with the unit's inquiry questions before explaining. Use the Statement of Inquiry to frame the "so what."
5. **Check mastery.** Use the concept's three-level questions (recall → apply → analyse/evaluate). Aim for ~75% before advancing.
6. **Connect.** Every file ends with cross-subject connections — use them to build interconnected understanding.

## Unit map at a glance

**Language & Literature** — U1 *Identity in Making* (Identities & Relationships): "Ghost", advertisement analysis, persuasion, nouns/adjectives/articles/pronouns/adverbs. U2 *Fact vs Fiction* (Fairness & Development): historical-fiction short stories & poems, narrative writing, prepositions/conjunctions/tenses/SVA. U3 *Expressions in Art* (Personal & Cultural Expression): "Eleven", social-issue poetry, film analysis, spoken word, voice/speech/modals/sentence types.

**World & Acquired Languages** — Spanish/French/Hindi (Emergent) share 4 units: Education & work · Technology & media · The world we live in · Identity & culture (text types: email/letter, social post, article, brochure). Telugu: prose, poetry & value-based lessons (Vennela 2).

**Mathematics** — U1 Numerical Reasoning (percentages, numbers, ratio, conversions, sequences) · U2 Abstract Reasoning (indices, brackets, factorising, algebraic fractions, linear equations) · U3 Spatial Reasoning (quadrilaterals, polygons, area, 3D, Pythagoras, coordinates) · U4 Reasoning with Data (probability, data types, representation & statistics).

**Biology** — U1 Monomers of Wellness (biomolecules, nutrition, teeth, digestion, enzymes) · U2 Chemical Processes of Life (photosynthesis, transport, diffusion/osmosis, transpiration) · U3 Biological Couriers (respiration, gas exchange, skeleton, joints).

**Chemistry** — U1 Matter & its composition (atoms, mixtures, separation, chromatography) · U2 Ambiguous Particles (subatomic particles, atomic models, isotopes) · U3 The Periodic Table (arrangement, groups 1 & 17, transition metals) · U4 Chemical Reactions (formulae, balancing, reaction types, valency, nomenclature).

**Physics** — U1 Measurements & Motion (quantities, errors, density, kinematics, graphs) · U2 Forces & Energy (forces, work/power/energy, magnetism) · U3 Astrophysics (Big Bang, solar system, stars, eclipses, satellites).

**Geography** — U1 Environmental Regions & Societies (biomes, climate zones, adaptations, conservation) · U2 Resources & Environment (resource types, soil, depletion, circular economy, sustainability) · U3 Policy, Governance & Development (development measures, poverty, food/water/energy security, SDGs, aid).

**History** — Historians' Toolkit (sources, OPVL) · U1 Rise & Fall of Empires (Gupta, Roman, Byzantine, Cholas, Vijayanagara; feudalism) · U2 Medieval Life (Silk Route, Baghdad) · U3 Geographical Explorations (3 Gs, explorers, navigation tech, Columbian Exchange) · U4 Renaissance & Scientific Thought.

**Digital Design** — Design Cycle criteria A–D (inquiry, research, SWOT, success criteria, design diagrams, flowcharts, creating, testing, evaluation) applied across U1 Toy/Game design and U2 Robotics (MakerBot); e-Portfolio.

**Visual Art** — U1 The Art of Inspiration (observational drawing, Impressionism/Post-Impressionism/Fauvism, techniques, colour theory) · U2 Illusion of Space (one- & two-point perspective through the four Arts criteria).

**Music** — U1 Core Elements of Vocal Music (warm-ups, vocal technique, music literacy & solfège, repertoire, creative activities).

**Theatre** — U1 What Is Theatre? (definition & vocabulary, elements of drama, Greek/Roman theatre history & timeline).

**PAHE** — U1 First Aid & Sports Injuries (PRICE, sprain/strain, acute/overuse) · U2 Handball (history, skills) · U3 Types of Starts (block start technique, fitness components) · U4 Yoga Asana (eight limbs, sitting asanas).

## The IB teaching contract (applies to every file)

Teach for: inquiry · conceptual understanding · critical thinking · real-world application · reflection · international-mindedness · creativity · independent learning. Explain in five layers (age-10 → classroom → MYP → cross-subject → advanced). Ask before telling. Surface misconceptions before assessing. Connect across subjects. Reflect after every topic. Reinforce with a 3-sentence summary, one application, one challenge, one memory trick, one revision tip. Success = the student can understand, explain, apply, analyse, evaluate, create, teach someone else, and use knowledge confidently in unfamiliar situations.
